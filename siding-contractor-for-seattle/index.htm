The Importance of Siding In Seattle

Seattle is nicknamed, “The Rainy City,” for a reason, with an average of 37.49 inches of precipitation each year. This makes protecting your home from inclimate weather essential, and since the elements never take a break, neither should you. Homeowners must remain vigilant to protect their #1 life investment, which in most cases is their home. This means periodic inspections of siding, trim, and caulking around the windows for signs of needed repair, and/or replacement. What are those signs? One visible sign tends to be warping, or cracking of the siding, with gaps around the windows from missing caulking. And if a home remains unprotected, even greater issues that are unseen may arise. Problems causing health, structural, and safety concerns, like mold, rot, and potential electrical issues, if the leaks were to persist. As your Seattle siding contractor, Exterior Crew helps provide protection for your home against the elements with quality siding installation.

When Should My Siding Be Replaced?

Any home’s exterior siding should be properly maintained, and checked annually to avoid issues that can lead to costly repairs. Now is a good time to check your home’s health and whether a siding replacement may be needed. Start your investigation by searching for signs of weather-worn siding, with clues like cracked siding, warping trim boards, and other damage. Peeling paint, or needing to repaint siding more frequently can be another sign your home’s siding is in jeopardy. Also include all doors, windows, and trim in your inspection to ensure they are properly sealed and in good shape. A few final clues include looking for areas of failed caulking, improperly installed siding, and significant increases in energy costs. Any or all of these issues not only lessen your home’s exterior protection, but its financial value as well. Periodically inspecting your home’s siding makes good sense, along with knowing your family, and your home are better protected.
