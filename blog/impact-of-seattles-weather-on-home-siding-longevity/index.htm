Impact of Seattle's Weather on Home Siding Longevity

by Exterior Crew for:
  • Residential Homeowners
← back to all blog posts

Seattle, often lovingly referred to as the Emerald City, offers breathtaking views, a rich culture, and, yes, its unique brand of weather. With its characteristic drizzles, gusty winds, and occasional sunbreaks, Seattle’s weather demands a distinct approach to home maintenance—especially concerning siding and its longevity.

The Weather Profile of Seattle

Seattle’s maritime climate gives it moderate temperatures but comes with abundant rainfall, especially during fall and winter. Unlike the extreme temperature variations seen in other parts of the country, Seattle’s weather predominantly revolves around rain, mist, and wind, with sunny days acting as the silver lining.

Common Types of Siding Materials

From classic wood to modern fiber cement, the siding landscape in Seattle homes is diverse.

Let’s take a quick look:

  • Wood Siding: Offers a timeless appeal and natural insulation.
  • Vinyl Siding: Low cost and low maintenance, it’s a common choice for many homeowners.
  • Metal Siding: Durable and fireproof, it’s less popular than it once was but still found on many homes.
  • Fiber Cement Siding: Resistant to termites and decay, this material offers the look of wood without the upkeep, making it the most popular choice among Emerald City homeowners.

Impact of Weather on Different Types of Siding

  • Wood Siding: The romance of wooden houses often fades with the appearance of rot and warping, courtesy of Seattle’s humidity. Prolonged moisture exposure can lead to mold and mildew. Furthermore, the limited sunny days can bleach and dry out the wood, making it brittle.
  • Vinyl Siding: While being relatively maintenance-free, vinyl siding in Seattle can suffer from color fading due to infrequent yet strong sun exposure. Heavy winds pose the risk of detaching or even damaging the vinyl panels.
  • Metal Siding: Though resilient, the humid coastal air can cause corrosion over time. Metal siding isn’t immune to dents either, which can result from wind-driven debris.
  • Fiber Cement Siding: A champion in the face of Seattle’s weather, fiber cement is not completely invincible. Extreme temperature fluctuations can lead to minor cracks. Over time, its color might also require a touch-up.

Preventive Measures for Extending Siding Longevity

  1. Material Selection – For Seattle’s wet climate, fiber cement and treated wood are excellent choices, offering longevity and reduced maintenance.
  2. Protective Coatings – Both wood and fiber cement siding can significantly benefit from protective coatings. Options such as sealants, primers, and paint, or even just a moisture-resistant treatment, can make a world of difference.
  3. Correct Installation – Ensure that siding is correctly installed. Proper overlapping, application of caulking, use of a moisture barrier, and secure fastening will prevent issues arising from wind and water infiltration.

Maintenance Tips

  1. Regular Inspection – Especially after stormy weather, inspect your siding for any signs of damage, wear, or detachment. Early detection can save you from huge repair bills in the future.
  2. Cleaning and Repairs – Regularly clean your siding to prevent mold and mildew, especially if you have wood siding. For minor damages, quick DIY repairs can prevent further deterioration.
  3. Professional Assistance – If the damage seems extensive, or you’re unsure about the state of your siding, don’t hesitate to consult with professionals from Exterior Crew. We will assess your home and provide a quote for any siding damage issues.

Seattle’s weather, as beautiful as it can be, comes with its set of challenges for homeowners. By understanding these challenges and taking proactive measures, you can ensure the longevity of your home’s siding, preserving both its functionality and aesthetic appeal.

 

FAQs

  • Q: How often should I repaint or reseal wood siding in Seattle?
    • A: Typically, every 5-7 years, but always monitor for signs of wear or damage.
  • Q: Can I use power washing for vinyl siding?
    • A: Yes, but use a low-pressure setting to prevent damage or water intrusion.
  • Q: What type of siding is most resistant to Seattle’s rainy climate?
    • A: Fiber cement siding is a top choice because of its resistance to moisture and decay.
  • Q: How can I detect early signs of water damage on my siding?
    • A: Look for signs like discoloration, peeling paint, soft spots on wood siding, and mold or mildew growth.
